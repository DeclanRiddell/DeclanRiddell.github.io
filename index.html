Patient Obesity Level Predictive Classification Model

The leading cause of death in the world is heart disease! Obesity is the leading risk of developing heart disease. Catching obesity early, and identifying leading factors in determining a patients obesity level or risk of obesity could lead to early therapeutic targeting which could help to stifle the leading cause of death across the world.

The goal of this project was to take a dataset reported by patients detailing their health scope. This included things from activity levels, family history, and diet. With this factor analysis I was aiming to find the most important of the groupings, whether that be physical, genetic, or dietary in order to determine the best course of treatment to reduce obesity level and help prevent heart disease.

Comprehensive Analysis of NGS Omics Data Reveals Distinct Gene Expression Profiles in Cancer Cell Lines K562 and MCF-7

Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) technologies have enabled the detailed examination of genomic landscapes, offering insights into the complex mechanisms of disease, particularly cancer. This study leverages NGS to analyze the gene expression profiles of two pivotal cancer cell lines: K562 (chronic myelogenous leukemia) and MCF-7 (breast cancer). Through rigorous data acquisition, quality control, reads alignment, mutation calling, gene expression analysis, and gene functional enrichment, we elucidate the molecular underpinnings that differentiate these cell lines. Our findings provide valuable insights into the gene expression dynamics and mutational landscapes characteristic of leukemia and breast cancer, contributing to the broader understanding of cancer biology and offering potential targets for therapeutic intervention

The advent of Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) technologies has profoundly impacted biomedical research, particularly in the study of cancer. By enabling comprehensive profiling of gene expression and genomic mutations, NGS offers unparalleled insights into the molecular mechanisms underlying cancer development and progression. This study focuses on the analysis of NGS data from two widely studied cancer cell lines: K562, a model for studying chronic myelogenous leukemia, and MCF-7, a model for breast cancer research. These cell lines represent distinct cancer types, providing a valuable opportunity to explore the molecular diversity underlying hematological and solid tumors

Tools: bowtie2, samtools, bcftools, macs2, cufflink/diff, IGV(Integrative Genomics Viewer)
